June 2022 FOMC

Treasury Curve Event Study

A static case study around the June 15, 2022 FOMC meeting, using pre-event and post-event Treasury curve snapshots plus 3D zero-rate and forward-rate surfaces.

Read Methodology Open Main Demo

Event Context

Event date: 2022-06-15. Pre-event curve date: 2022-06-14. Post-event curve date: 2022-06-16. After the meeting, the federal funds target range was 1.50% to 1.75%.

The on-the-run U.S. Treasury curve is used here as a liquid USD government benchmark and an approximate risk-free curve for rates-risk visualization. It is not a perfect pure risk-free curve because Treasury yields can also reflect liquidity, term premia, supply-demand effects, and inflation expectations.

Pre/Post Treasury Curve

The pre/post chart compares the selected maturity grid immediately before and after the FOMC decision.

Curve Move In Basis Points

A flat curve-move chart would indicate a parallel shift. Uneven maturity-by-maturity moves indicate a non-parallel shift.

3D Curve Surfaces

Level, Slope, And Curvature

Level is the average of the 2Y, 5Y, 10Y, and 30Y points. Slope is 10Y minus 2Y. Curvature is 2 times 5Y minus 2Y minus 10Y.

Desk-Quant Interpretation

The case study frames the meeting as a rates-risk shock rather than a single-price event. The pre/post curve chart shows how the curve level changed, while the basis-point move chart separates parallel from non-parallel behavior. The 3D surfaces show whether the event shift was isolated to one date or part of a broader June curve repricing.

For desk risk, the non-parallel pattern matters because a portfolio with concentrated 2Y, 5Y, 10Y, or 30Y key-rate exposure can gain or lose even when the headline level move looks modest. The forward surface adds another lens by showing how the event changed implied rates between maturity buckets.
